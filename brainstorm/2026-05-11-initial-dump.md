## Working Premise

A society bans job-replacing AI after mass labor disruption. Decades later, demographic collapse creates a healthcare and eldercare crisis, forcing governments to reopen AI use in medicine. Medical AI develops sensory therapies for dementia, sleep, memory, and behavioral regulation. These therapies accidentally reveal that perception can act like a command interface for the brain and body. Over time, beneficial sensory medicine mutates into memotoxins: perception-based hazards that can injure or kill.

## Pushbacks raised in iteration 1

Four consistency / plausibility issues raised:

1. The "AI ban preserved jobs and was popular" claim is weak — if demographic collapse arrived anyway, the ban didn't save anyone. **Resolution**: ban is *politically locked, not popular*. Auditing bureaucracy is a major employer constituency. Displacement generation still votes. Nobody can unwind it.

2. Why does AI uniquely discover stimulus-injection? Neuroscience studied multimodal binding for a century. **Resolution**: medical AI in eldercare runs de facto closed-loop combinatorial optimization on millions of monitored patients — discovery emerges from scale no IRB would approve for human researchers.

3. Single-image memotoxins by 2077, story starts 2080 — too fast. **Resolution**: pushed compression timeline far back. Story era is firmly three-component. Two-component is bleeding-edge classified. Single-component is theoretical.

4. First public attack logistics didn't work — same population needs all three components. **Resolution**: image component via mass broadcast (hijacked livestream / viral ad), scent and tone via chokepoints (transit hubs, broadcasts where exposed people concentrate). Attacker doesn't need 100% completion; 2% mass casualty is catastrophic.

## New concepts introduced in iteration 1

- **Exposure classification**: Clean / Marked / Twice-Marked / Sealed / Bound (Classes 0–4).
- **Sealed** = all three experienced, binding incomplete. The precarious state.
- **Cognitive Dilution Protocols**: deliberate impairment of memory consolidation to prevent binding. Clinical version: sleep fragmentation, anticholinergics, alcohol windows. Folk version: stink dens, pattern bars, drinking culture.
- **Sensory Saturation**: flood channels with chaotic variants to prevent specific patterns from achieving binding salience.
- **Counter-intuition**: vulnerability inversely correlates with cognitive sharpness. Attentive, well-rested, high-fidelity-memory people are most at risk.
- **Sudden street deaths**: Bound people triggered by partial cues weeks or months after encoding. Common enough by 2080 to be procedural.

# Inspo — Claude's drafts of the 13 stories

These are **inspiration drafts** of the twelve stories that aren't already written (FILE 006, The Censor's Hum, is the user's own draft and lives in `web-book/src/content/files/the-censors-hum.mdx`).

Use them however serves the work:
- Read them and rewrite each story in your own voice
- Use them as scaffolding — keep the structure, replace the prose
- Use them as inspiration for a single beat or phrase
- Ignore them and start fresh

Each draft honors the canon as of November 2026: dates, character names, the four locked cross-story connections, the archival-header framing, the voice registers from `world/registers.md`.

## Things worth knowing about the drafts

- **The Marina arc (009→010→011) is the emotional spine.** I let it run slightly longer than strictly necessary; she needs the room to be liked before she breaks.

- **FILE 011 (The Document) is intentionally short.** The site renders the blackout. The prose's job is to deliver Marina to the door of the document and end. The archival-note coda at the end of the draft is meant to feel like the reconstructor (Wei) cataloguing what was lost.

- **FILE 004 (The Memo) is structurally different.** It is the document itself, with redactions baked into the prose. The Voss handwritten note is the Vellum-thread reveal. The Archivist's note at the end is the only thing that breaks frame.

- **FILE 005 (Black Bloom) leans into the canon mechanism the most explicitly.** The first half walks the reader through how exposure actually works — image, then scent, then sound. This is the book's one place where the mechanism is rendered as story, not concept.

- **FILE 007 (Bound) is the most experimental voice.** Anna's interior is partly broken; the prose drifts; the moment where Lihua's son is referenced without naming is the only place the four locked connections meet on the page within a single story.

- **FILE 008 (The Boy Who Hummed) ends with a structural twist** — Ranjit's note suggests the phenomenon may be *older* than canon publicly acknowledges (post-viral encoding from NCV-34 survivors in the 2040s). This is consistent with the timeline but expands what the OMC believes. Use or cut as fits your direction.

- **FILE 013 (The Archivist) is the only story without an archival header.** Wei speaks directly. The reveal lands gradually rather than as a twist. The last section — *"There is a thing I have to say about the cooperation thesis"* — is the place where the collection's theme is stated as plainly as it will ever be stated. Cut that section if you want the theme to land in subtext only.

## What I'd do differently if I were doing this again

- I would write the Bound (007) story before the Marina trilogy, to test the lyrical-fragmented register and decide whether to push others in that direction.
- I would draft Black Bloom (005) twice: once present-tense visceral, once retrospective from her partner's POV. Pick the better one.
- I would consider folding Marina at the Window (010) and Marina's Refusal (009) into a single longer story. The two-story split is structurally clean but pacing-wise might be tighter as one piece.
- The Designer (012) should probably go *slightly* shorter. Karl's monologue risks being indulgent toward the end.

These are notes, not corrections. Trust your own instincts when you rewrite.

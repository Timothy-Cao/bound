# Technology and Countermeasures

## Personal protective tech

- **AR censor goggles**, Worn devices that blur dense visual textures, flatten stereogram depth, desaturate risky colors, blank unknown imagery, and label unsafe surfaces. Quality varies wildly by price.
- **Scent masks**, Filtered respirators. Mandatory in some workplaces, common in transit.
- **Audio scrubbers**, Active noise filters that cancel or smear suspect patterns. Hidden in earbuds. Interfere with speech and alarms; trade-off is real.
- **Safe-mode phones**, Default UI loads images as descriptions; user must opt in to view.
- **Visual blur filters**, Software-level filtering on personal screens.
- **Pattern flatteners**, Specialized hardware that processes incoming video to remove depth illusions.
- **Low-fidelity cameras**, Bodycams, dashcams, and most consumer recording defaults to safe-mode capture.

## Environmental tech

- **Scent-neutral HVAC**, Filtration designed to strip suspect scent compounds from building air. Standard in offices, hospitals, courts, transit hubs.
- **Hazard-scanning doorways**, Detect suspect visual/scent/audio profiles on entry.
- **Visual-density meters**, Public sensors flagging high-pattern-load environments.
- **Smell detectors**, Olfactory analyzers in transit and government buildings.
- **Safe perfume certification**, Legal scheme certifying compounds with no known memotoxin overlap.
- **Low-risk architecture**, Building standards: matte surfaces, soft geometry, controlled lighting, predictable scent.

## Defensive AI

- **Censor AI**, Trained on the Restricted Stimulus Registry to detect hazardous patterns in real time. Used in clearance pipelines, media production, public-screen filtering.
- **Contamination risk**, Per **R8**: censor AI has, by definition, encoded the patterns it blocks. Several documented cases of censor systems generating hazardous outputs through subtle adversarial pressure. Major censor systems are now air-gapped and require human review of their outputs.

## Sensory Denial Protocols (SDP)

The most consequential clinical intervention developed in the 2070s. SDP is the elective ablation of a sensory channel as defense against memotoxin completion in Twice-Marked patients. See `canon/world-rules.md` R14 and `canon/glossary.md` for definitions.

**OAP (Olfactory Ablation)** is the most commonly performed. Olfaction is the most survivable sense to lose; humans live, eat, work, love without smell. The procedure is a brief surgical destruction of the olfactory bulb, sometimes done under local anesthesia. Recovery is fast. The long-term costs are real but manageable for the wealthy: dietary changes, scent-monitoring household devices, social adjustment.

**CAP (Cochlear Ablation)** is rare. Deafness is a severe quality-of-life consequence in 2080 (audio scrubbers are everywhere; deafness compounds the existing alienation). Reserved for patients with no acceptable alternative.

**Visual Ablation** is almost theoretical. Fewer than a hundred performed globally.

**The Anosmia Paradox**: ablation does not protect against LCI completion. The brain reconstructs the missing sense from memory, and the binding fires anyway. By 2080, ablation has become *less effective than it was in the 2070s*. The medical community has not openly conceded this. Patients ablated in 2073 against fresh-component risks are now dying of LCI completions; survivors of the ablation feel betrayed.

## Mass screening and biometric monitoring

Population-scale screening for early Marked status was attempted in several jurisdictions between 2076 and 2079. The programs collapsed for three reasons:

1. Volume: the systems were overwhelmed by the number of positive results, most of them representing LCI components in the general population (see *Diagnostic Limits*, below).
2. False sense of security: the screens caught only registered patterns; people screened "Clean" turned out to be Marked by unregistered components.
3. Civil liberties: mandatory screening was politically untenable in most democratic jurisdictions.

What remains:

- **Cognitive Exposure Passports** (2078): voluntary or employer-mandated tracking of registered exposure
- **Wealthy individuals**: continuous biometric monitoring via wearables and (for the very wealthy) implants
- **Bound-care clinics**: passive observation of patients with confirmed Class 3 or 4 status
- **No universal screening**: the dream of population-scale early detection has died

## Diagnostic limits (canonical)

The classification system is fundamentally aspirational. It tracks what the system can see, not what is actually happening in patients' brains.

- **Class 4 (Bound)**: clinical, certain. The patient is in or has been in cascade.
- **Class 3 (Sealed)**: usually inferred from near-miss events or registry-confirmed exposure to all three known components. Reliable for known memotoxin patterns.
- **Class 2 (Twice-Marked)**: inferred from registry-confirmed exposure to two known components. *High false-negative rate* because the registry catches only known patterns.
- **Class 1 (Marked)**: inferred similarly. Even higher false-negative rate.
- **Class 0 (Clean)**: essentially unconfirmable. People are presumed Clean if they have no registered exposure. Most "Clean" classifications are wrong; the person is actually Marked or Twice-Marked by unregistered component combinations.

This is the dark underside of the Passport system: most people walking around classified Clean are not actually clean. They are Marked or Twice-Marked by mundane unknown combinations they encountered in food, public spaces, household products, and inherited cultural artifacts. The Verrick broadcast attack confirmed this publicly: thousands of "Clean" Passport-holders died from a single newly-deployed visual component combining with legacy components they had carried for decades without knowing.

The OMC has not formally adjusted the classification system in response. The system continues to issue Class 0 designations to people who are almost certainly not Class 0. Reform proposals have stalled.

## Extinction therapy and selective component disruption

Experimental clinical practices, partial efficacy:

- **Extinction therapy**: deliberate repeated exposure to *variants* of a known component to degrade the original encoding below the binding threshold. Sometimes works on visual components. Less reliable for scent.
- **Selective neural intervention**: targeted disruption of a single encoded memory using transcranial magnetic stimulation or experimental optogenetics. Sometimes successful; sometimes destroys other memories.
- **Sleep-deprivation maintenance**: continuous partial sleep deprivation in Sealed patients to prevent consolidation. Severe quality-of-life cost. Limited to high-risk patients.

None of these is reliable enough for mass deployment. Each is offered at well-resourced clinics for patients who can pay.

## Clinical tech

- **Bound-care clinics**, Facilities specializing in Class 3/4 management. Controlled environments, dilution protocols, palliative care for terminal Bound patients.
- **Anti-recall therapy**, Experimental: targeted neural disruption of suspected bound memories. Mixed results; sometimes successful, sometimes catastrophic.
- **Memory quarantine apps**, Personal tools that log suspected exposures and prompt dilution practices.
- **Cognitive exposure passports**, Personal records of exposure history. Used by employers, insurers, and dating apps. Legally optional but socially required in many contexts.
- **Hazard insurance**, Coverage for exposure events, bound-care, and survivor benefits. Major industry.

## Why countermeasures fail (per R6)

- **Goggles can be removed.** People do this for intimacy, art, religion, rebellion, eating, sex, sleep.
- **Filters only catch known patterns.** New variants slip through until they're catalogued.
- **Overfiltering causes false positives.** Cheap goggles blur faces, traffic, alarms. Safety equipment becomes its own hazard.
- **Scent masks are uncomfortable.** Long-term wear causes skin issues and social friction.
- **Audio filters interfere with speech.** People miss important conversations and warnings.
- **Hazards mutate.** New memotoxins appear faster than detection catches up.
- **Generated memotoxins have no signature.** Not yet a major problem in 2080, but classified state systems are getting there.
- **Bound victims can be triggered later.** No filter on Earth protects a Class 4 person from the smell of bread.

## The AR goggles experience

Most middle-class adults wear them in public, off at home. A few patterns are universal:

- **Standard mode**: blurs the worst, lets through faces and text. Comfortable.
- **Crowd mode**: more aggressive; assumes you're in a high-risk space.
- **Hard safe mode**: maximum filtering. Faces become smooth ovals, walls become flat planes, no art is visible. Used by the traumatized, the paranoid, witnesses under threat, and (paradoxically) the very wealthy who can afford private cleared spaces where they don't need to remove the goggles.
- **Cheap-goggle failure modes**: lag, overblur, false-positive on traffic lights, occasional crashes that drop you into raw vision in a public space. Working-class anxiety.

## Hard-safe-mode subculture

People who live in hard safe mode full-time. They cannot see faces; they identify people by voice or AR-overlaid name tags. Their world is smooth and labeled. Some are survivors. Some are religious. Some are fashion-forward. A small but real subculture.

# Cascading Effects

The memotoxin world's primary mechanisms (defenses, regulations, social practices, markets) generate enormous secondary consequences. This file traces them by domain. Use this for texture, character backgrounds, and scene-grounding.

## Childhood and development

### The goggle generation
Children born after 2073 have worn AR goggles since roughly age four. The consequences are showing up in late adolescence and early adulthood (the eldest are now seventeen in 2080):

- **Reduced face-recognition skill.** Goggles preserve faces but at lower visual fidelity. Kids who grow up in standard-mode goggles read faces by voice-confidence and posture more than by feature. Adults who grew up pre-2073 find this generation's social attention disorienting.
- **Emotional recognition impairment.** Some research (still contested) shows reduced ability to read micro-expressions, sarcasm, ambivalence. Concern about empathy development.
- **Saccade-disruption neurological costs.** Children in Gen-4 saccade goggles report higher rates of headache, mild vestibular instability, and motor coordination delays. The "goggle headache" is a generational complaint. Long-term consequences unknown — the oldest affected children are still teenagers.
- **Visual literacy collapse.** Pattern-heavy art, dense imagery, and complex visual culture are filtered out of childhood. Young adults can't read paintings, struggle with diagrams, find non-flat visuals genuinely difficult.
- **Compensatory verbal development.** Kids talk more, are more verbose, more explicitly descriptive. Language carries what visuals can't. Vocabulary metrics in this generation are sharply up.
- **Touch and voice fetishization.** With visual channels filtered, touch and voice carry more weight. Adolescent intimacy is more tactile and more verbal than visual.

### Children's safety training and the imagination problem
The kindergarten rule — *eyes down, ears covered, breath held* — produces predictable outcomes:

- **Anxious relationship to perception itself.** Children learn early that the world can hurt them just by being looked at. A meaningful fraction develop perceptual anxiety symptoms by adolescence.
- **Informant culture in families.** Kids are taught to report repeated dreams and unknown songs to adults. They are also taught that adults will sometimes report them to clinics. Family trust suffers.
- **Imagination is suspect.** Drawing strange things, describing made-up monsters, telling fairy tales with rich imagery — all flagged. Children who draw too much get tested.
- **Storytelling impoverishment.** Fairy tales have been heavily pruned. Modern children's literature is famously plain. A generation grew up without dragons.
- **Describing is encoding.** Children learn very early that words carry sensory content. A child who describes a "strange image they saw" can encode it in a listening sibling. This shapes how kids talk about their inner lives — they learn not to.
- **Generational personality.** Quiet, watchful, suspicious of novelty. Adults who grew up before 2070 find this generation's reserve unsettling.

### Birthday parties, friendships, and play
Home parties almost vanished after 2076 — too risky to invite a child of unknown exposure history into a private home. Commercial cleared venues took over. Consequences:

- **Friendship slow to form.** Kids' social circles are family + school. New friendships through "playdates" require parental screening — exposure history exchange, then visit, then maybe friendship.
- **Smaller circles.** Average child knows fewer peers, more deeply.
- **Peer culture moves through text/voice.** Visual media-sharing among kids is heavily regulated. Adolescent friendship runs on long voice exchanges, written notes, shared songs (cleared).

### Birth rates and parenting culture
Birth rates remain low (extension of SES). Parenting has intensified — fewer children, more invested, more monitored:

- **Childhood exposure logging** as a wealthy-class status practice. Logged children get access to school admissions, insurance, employment that unlogged children don't.
- **Hereditary defense.** Wealthy families pass defensive practices that working-class families can't. Class lock-in begins at infancy.
- **Migration for childhood safety.** Some parents move to "clean districts" or religious communities for their kids' protection — practical conversions.

## Health and iatrogenic damage

### Clinical Cognitive Dilution side effects
Long-term CDP regimens produce predictable harm. The treatment is, in some cases, doing more cumulative damage than untreated binding risk would have:

- **Sleep fragmentation** → chronic exhaustion, mood disorders, cognitive decline at younger ages than expected.
- **Anticholinergic exposure** → measurable elevation in dementia risk in long-term patients. (Real-world finding, deployed seriously here.) Iatrogenic decline becomes its own public health crisis.
- **Benzodiazepine windows** → dependency in a meaningful minority of patients.
- **Some Class 3 patients deteriorate faster from the protection than they would have from the binding.** A live medical-ethics debate. Doctors quietly refer to patients in this state as "saved into dying."

### Folk dilution side effects
The folk path is no kinder:

- **Alcoholism rates surge.** A generation drinks nightly as a casual health measure. Liver disease, cardiovascular damage, domestic violence, accident rates all up.
- **Children of folk-dilution households** grow up with parents who drink every night as a *health practice*. The messaging is confusing: drinking is protective, drinking is a problem, drinking is normal. Adolescent alcohol use in folk-dilution communities is unusually high and unusually accepted.
- **Stink dens are still bars** with all the usual problems.
- **Pattern bar regulars** face periodic police harassment, criminalization, occasional violence.

### Scent loss and food culture
Scent-neutral regulation has gutted entire food traditions:

- **Heavy-scent cuisines** (curry, garlic, intense herbs, fermentation) class-marked as "rural," "dirty," or "old country." Class signal.
- **Flavor flattening** — mainstream restaurant food is olfactorily plain. Critics complain. Diners adapt.
- **Coffee, wine, tea cultures** moved to private clubs and licensed venues. Casual neighborhood cafés stopped being possible.
- **Cooking-at-home** declined as standard scent-neutral HVAC made aromatic cooking awkward in apartment buildings.

### Mental health
The cumulative atmosphere has measurable mental-health costs:

- **Perceptual anxiety disorders** — a new diagnostic category by 2076. Patients experience normal perception as threatening.
- **Hard-safe-mode dependency** — people who can no longer tolerate unfiltered vision. A small but growing population.
- **Trust collapse** — generalized inability to trust strangers, environments, media. Increasing.
- **Loneliness epidemic** continues from SES. The defenses against memotoxins reinforce the conditions that produced SES in the first place.

## Privacy, surveillance, and the passport regime

### Cognitive Exposure Passports — second-order effects
Beyond the technical defense (Gen 10), passports reshape ordinary life:

- **Insurance and employment** tied to passport state. People with too many encoded components become uninsurable, unemployable in safe environments.
- **Dating apps** integrate passport data. Some explicitly filter by exposure profile.
- **Education access.** Elite schools require clean passports. Wealthy parents log infants to keep options open.
- **Mortgage and housing** — some buildings will not rent to high-exposure tenants.
- **"Passport bankruptcy"** — a legal and social state where a person has accumulated so many encoded components that they cannot work in cleared environments. Often the result of one prolonged exposure incident. Significant subculture of passport-bankrupt people working in unregulated industries.
- **The undocumented** — immigrants, the homeless, refugees from unbanned states, the rural poor — live without passport infrastructure. Unemployable in safe sectors. Effectively a separate caste.

### Black market in passports
Whole industry around modifying, faking, or wiping passport data. Risky — caught modifications carry severe penalties — but lucrative. Some wealthy people buy clean passport histories from people willing to take on the encoded exposure burden (and the resulting career constraints) in exchange for cash. A modern form of indentured exposure.

## Economic and labor effects

### The auditing class
The AI Ban auditing and clearance bureaucracy is ~8–10M jobs globally. It is:

- The largest single employer in many countries.
- The most politically powerful constituency. The reason the Ban cannot be unwound.
- A self-perpetuating bureaucracy whose interests are not those of the broader economy.
- A class with distinct culture, slang, dress (conservative, scent-neutral, goggle-modest).

### Insurance industry boom
Sensory hazard insurance is now larger than auto insurance in many markets:

- Standard household policies include hazard riders.
- Event insurance for weddings, concerts, conferences is a major sector.
- Bound-care life insurance (paying out as soon as Class 3 status is confirmed) is a controversial product.
- Insurance investigators are a significant employment category — they verify exposure claims, sometimes by hunting down components on the black market.

### Geographic class sorting
Sensory safety has produced geographic stratification beyond historical class lines:

- **Clean districts** — neighborhoods with consistent low-density visual environment, controlled scent, certified media access. Premium real estate.
- **Mixed districts** — middle class, decent goggles, partial protection.
- **Saturation districts** — poor neighborhoods, cheap goggles, high latent component exposure, high rate of sudden street death.
- **Migration patterns** — internal refugees from saturation districts seeking safer areas. Clean districts respond with restrictive entry policies, sometimes explicit, sometimes covert.

### The scent-chemist economy
With only ~200 chemists worldwide capable of binding-grade scent work, ~12 doing contract work:

- **Surveillance** of all 200 is essentially total in banned states.
- **Recruitment competition** between SSDO, corporate gray-market, terror groups.
- **Children of chemists** have been targeted for kidnapping, both by attackers (for leverage) and by defenders (for protective custody).
- **Trauma rates** among practicing chemists are extreme. Drug and alcohol dependency common.
- **Witness protection ex-chemists** form a tiny subculture, often relocated multiple times.

## Family and intimacy

### Marriage and household formation
Pre-marriage exposure-history disclosure is now standard in many cultures. The negotiations are intricate:

- Wealthy families exchange comprehensive logs going back to childhood.
- Working-class couples exchange less but still discuss.
- Some communities have professional matchmakers who specialize in exposure-history compatibility.
- "Bound-status disclosure" is legally required before marriage in most jurisdictions.
- Marriages between high-exposure and low-exposure partners can produce insurance complications, employment issues for the household.

### Sex and intimacy
Goggles off, in private, is the modern equivalent of getting fully naked. Real intimacy:

- Couples gradually share unfiltered media. "Showing you something cleared" is courtship; "showing you something uncleared" is profound trust.
- Some couples never view each other unfiltered.
- Pornography is heavily regulated; underground unfiltered material is a major black market.

### Childhood and dependents
Already covered above; one more note:

- **Multi-generational households** are more common again. Grandparents who lived pre-2070 can teach defensive practices that parents born after don't know.
- **Tension between generations** — older relatives find the new caution unbearable; younger relatives find older relatives' habits dangerous (perfumes, music, decor).

## Religion and community demographics

R12 has reshuffled religion:

- **Orthodox / Plain / monastic traditions** are growing, partly because their practice is protective. Conversion driven by practicality rather than faith is a major pastoral concern.
- **"Defense converts"** — middle-class educated people joining strict religious communities largely for protection. Some communities welcome them; others view them with suspicion as potential leakers.
- **Cult susceptibility** rises in populations exposed to bad-faith protective claims. Several minor cults exploit defense desperation.
- **Mainstream churches losing members** to stricter traditions or to none. Pews thinning.
- **Religious authorities** in protective traditions have become disproportionately influential because their membership has become disproportionately functional and prosperous.

## What this generation looks like as adults

The first cohort raised entirely under post-2073 conditions is hitting young adulthood now (2080). The composite:

- Quiet, verbal, touch-attentive.
- Anxious about novelty, especially sensory novelty.
- Verbose, descriptive, articulate about inner states.
- Visually impoverished — can't read paintings, find unfiltered media overwhelming.
- Comfortable with regulation, surveillance, exposure tracking — it's what they've always known.
- Strong friendships within small circles, weak engagement with broader society.
- High alcohol use, normalized.
- Low birth rate continuing.
- Increasingly drawn to religious or community structures that offer protection.
- Resentful of the goggle headache and the social cost of growing up filtered, but unable to imagine alternatives.

This is the generation our protagonists are likely from, or are likely shaping for.

# Institutions and Law

## Major laws

- **AI Labor Ban (2031)**, Bars AI from replacing human jobs except in narrow emergency cases. Still the foundational economic statute.
- **Healthcare AI Exception Act (2050)**, The wedge. Permits medical AI under licensing.
- **Cognitive Rehabilitation Authorization (2057)**, Legal category that opened deep memory/mood/perception research.
- **Sensory Safety Act (2073)**, Comprehensive regulation of cognitively active media.
- **Public Scent Ordinances**, Municipal patchwork; varies wildly by city.
- **Certified Display Standards**, Standards for what public screens may show.
- **Cognitively Active Media Licensing**, Required to produce/distribute regulated media categories.
- **Hazardous Stimulus Possession Law**, Criminalizes possession of restricted stimulus material.
- **Media Liability Law**, Holds platforms liable for distribution of uncleared content.
- **Emergency Grayout Protocol**, Authorizes blanket shutdown of public displays in suspected attacks.
- **Restricted Stimulus Export Ban**, Treaty-level controls; weakly enforced internationally.
- **Bound Witness Protocols (2076)**, Special legal framework for Class 4 individuals exposed to classified patterns.

## Major institutions

- **Department of Perceptual Health (DPH)**, Federal-level public health for stimulus injection. Manages classification, screening, clinical guidelines, exposure registries.
- **Office of Memetic Containment**, Inside DPH. Operational response to detected hazards. Has its own field agents.
- **Restricted Stimulus Registry**, Classified database of known memotoxin patterns. Censor AI training data ultimately derives from this. Leaks are catastrophic.
- **Sensory Incident Response Bureau (SIRB)**, First-responder agency for suspected attacks. Coordinates with local "Stim Response" police units.
- **Cognitive Rehabilitation Authority**, Licensing body for sensory therapy practice.
- **Public Media Clearance Board**, Issues green-tags for media.
- **Strategic Stimulus Defense Office (SSDO)**, Military / intelligence research. Officially defensive. Realistically: an offensive memotoxin program with deniability.
- **Bound Witness Protection Program**, Handles Class 4 individuals with classified knowledge. Houses them in controlled environments, sometimes for life.

## Bureaucratic conflict

The institutional landscape is not unified. Several factions actively work at cross-purposes:

- **Health agencies (DPH)** want sensory therapy preserved and bound-care expanded. They view memotoxins as a manageable public health problem.
- **Labor groups** fear that any expansion of healthcare AI re-opens the wider AI ban question. Will block almost anything.
- **Security agencies (SSDO, intelligence services)** want better weapons. Their defensive research is offensive research with a disclaimer. They have classified access to memotoxin material that DPH does not.
- **Civil rights groups** oppose sensory surveillance, exposure registries, and mandatory screening.
- **Religious groups** oppose regulation of incense, chants, icons, ritual space.
- **Corporations** lobby for "subclinical" stimulus influence in advertising, productivity, and consumer products. Some of this is legal. Some is regulatory gray. Some is overt violation.

## Government stance vs. reality

**Publicly**: memotoxin research is illegal except for defense and medicine, under strict oversight.

**Privately**: state actors maintain active offensive research programs. They justify this as necessary for detection (you can't defend against what you don't know how to make). Defensive research slowly, inevitably, becomes offensive capability.

The story-relevant question by 2080 is not whether the state has offensive memotoxin capability, it does, but whether anyone outside the SSDO knows the full scope, and what happens when someone leaks.

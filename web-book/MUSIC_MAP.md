# Music Map

Maps audio files to chapters and documents what each track sounds like.

## Tracks

### After Hours Annex.mp3 / After Hours Annex (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** Slow institutional drone. Empty government building after hours. Fluorescent hum, distant ventilation, hollow marble reverb. A single low piano note struck once and left to decay. Analog tape warble creeping in at the edges. Bureaucratic dread. No rhythm, no melody.
- **Style:** dark ambient, institutional, analog decay, cold war, minimal
- **Vibe:** Cold bureaucratic emptiness, post-session government building
- **Chapters:** The Vote (FILE 001), The Memo (FILE 004)

### After The Vote.mp3
- **Prompt:** Recovered archival audio fragment. Degraded analog tape recording from a government hearing chamber, circa 2035. The ambient sound of a large institutional room — distant murmuring voices, the creak of wooden benches, papers shuffling. A gavel strikes once, sharply. The murmuring swells briefly, then dies into an unnatural stillness. Faint electrical hum from old fluorescent lighting. A long pause. Then the sound of rain against tall windows, muffled and steady, growing slowly louder as the room empties. Footsteps receding on marble. The recording degrades — tape warble, static crackle, dropout artifacts. The rain continues underneath the distortion, then cuts to silence. Lo-fi, archival quality. No music. No narration. Documentary realism with an undercurrent of dread. Duration: 60–90 seconds.
- **Style:** archival, lo-fi, documentary realism, dread
- **Vibe:** The moment after the AI Labor Ban passes — the gallery empties, rain on windows, tape decay
- **Chapters:** The Vote (FILE 001)

### Late Afternoon Kitchen.mp3 / Late Afternoon Kitchen (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** Intimate domestic ambient. A kitchen in late afternoon. Warm but uneasy stillness. Faint wind outside a closed window. A low chord held on strings, barely audible breathing room. Something gentle that feels like it's about to break. Tape hiss underneath. No percussion.
- **Style:** ambient, intimate, melancholy, lo-fi, warm analog, still
- **Vibe:** Domestic warmth with underlying fragility — a home that isn't safe anymore
- **Chapters:** Shabbat in 2042 (FILE 002), Marina's Refusal (FILE 009), Marina at the Window (FILE 010)

### Off Key Hum.mp3 / Off Key Hum (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** A human voice humming a simple melody, distorted through layers of analog tape degradation. The hum is calm but wrong — slightly off-pitch, repeating without variation, as if the person doesn't know they're doing it. Underneath: low sub-bass drone, faint electrical interference. The hum gets closer. Unsettling. Hypnotic.
- **Style:** dark ambient, vocal drone, analog horror, lo-fi, hypnotic, unsettling
- **Vibe:** The memotoxin made audible — involuntary, contagious humming
- **Chapters:** The Censor's Hum (FILE 006), The Boy Who Hummed (FILE 008)

### Sterile Platform Tone.mp3 / Sterile Platform Tone (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** A transit station in the morning. Distant crowd murmur dissolving into silence. A tone emerges — not music, a frequency. Clinical and piercing. Heart monitor rhythm buried deep in the mix, slowing. Sterile air. Everything bright and wrong. The frequency overtakes all other sound. Abrupt tape cut.
- **Style:** dark ambient, clinical, dystopian, horror drone, sterile, industrial
- **Vibe:** The Black Bloom exposure — a public space turning lethal, clinical dread
- **Chapters:** Black Bloom (FILE 005), First Cardiac Event (FILE 003)

### Confiscated Signal.mp3 / Confiscated Signal (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** Deep sub-bass pressure. The sound of being in a sealed room with no windows. Total silence broken by faint magnetic tape flutter. A low chorus of processed voices, indistinct, as if redacted. Growing weight and claustrophobia. No melody, no rhythm — just mass. The air itself feels classified. Ends with a hard stop, like a recording being confiscated.
- **Style:** drone, oppressive, redacted, dark ambient, subterranean, classified
- **Vibe:** Sealed containment — the weight of something you're not allowed to know
- **Chapters:** The Document (FILE 011), Bound (FILE 007)

### Last Tape Room.mp3 / Last Tape Room (1).mp3
- **Prompt:** A single person alone with a recording device. Room tone of a small empty space. Faint tape motor whir. A sparse, broken piano — two or three notes, wide apart, no pattern. The feeling of someone about to speak but not yet speaking. Regret and exhaustion. The tape runs out. Static.
- **Style:** ambient, solo piano, lo-fi tape, confessional, sparse, end-of-life
- **Vibe:** A final confession recorded to no one — the designer and the archivist alone with what they've done
- **Chapters:** The Designer (FILE 012), The Archivist (FILE 013)

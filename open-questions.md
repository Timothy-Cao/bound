## Worldbuilding gaps that don't block writing

These can sit unresolved indefinitely. Develop a specific answer only when a specific story needs one.

### International situation
The unbanned states are background tension throughout the collection. Two questions left open:
1. Should any specific country be named? (Currently: unnamed.) Naming would commit the story to a geopolitical specificity it doesn't need.
2. Do they ever become an active plot threat by 2080, or stay background? (Currently: background only. No story directly dramatizes them.)

**Recommendation**: leave both unnamed and undramatized unless a drafted story finds it can't avoid them.

### Vellum cult specifics
We have the Vellum as a named cult-terrorist group in [`world/actors-and-motives.md`](world/actors-and-motives.md). We don't have:
- A named doctrine or text
- A specific historical incident attributed to them in canon

**Recommendation**: develop only what a story requires. If FILE 011 or FILE 012 needs to name a Vellum operative, name them then.

### State treatment of Bound witnesses to classified attacks
Currently in [`open-questions.md`](open-questions.md) as Q5 of the older list, what happens to Class 4 individuals who witnessed classified material? Permanent protective custody? Witness protection? Quiet euthanasia? Aggressive anti-recall? My standing answer: *all four happen, depending on classification level and political moment.* Ambiguous by design.

### The Archivist's foreword vs. final piece
Wei Han potentially appears in two places: the foreword (an unsigned reconstructor's note) and FILE 013 (signed, named). Open question: should the foreword *also* be Wei in disguise (anonymous-feeling but actually them), or should the foreword be deliberately voiceless (institutional, suggesting an editor) so FILE 013's reveal lands harder?

**My lean**: deliberately voiceless foreword. Makes FILE 013 a true reveal.

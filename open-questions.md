These are the live questions paused at the end of the last working session. When resuming, work through these in order. The plot direction is captured in [`plot/the-book.md`](plot/the-book.md).

### R1. The archival-reconstruction frame — yes or no?
The most consequential pending decision. Should the book be framed as a posthumous reconstruction, with chapters interleaved with redacted documents, transcripts, biometric logs, declassified memos? Light touch (occasional fragments) or heavy (every chapter integrated with archival material)?

**My lean**: yes, light touch. Most chapters are normal limited third; intermittent archival fragments break up the flow and increase in density toward the climax. Models: the dossier sections in *World War Z*, the redacted documents in *The Power* — not full *House of Leaves*.

**If yes**: the page-of-text climax becomes structurally inevitable. The reconstructor is the implicit narrator and an interesting late-book question (who is reconstructing, why?).

**If no**: the page-of-text climax still works but lands more as a stylistic intervention than a structural payoff. Fine, just different.

### R2. Is the chemist's self-deception the book's spine?
The chemist investigating her own role in the contract she "refused" could be the central mystery, with the broader memotoxin plot wrapped around it. Her death at the page-of-text climax becomes the moment she and the reader simultaneously realize what she did.

**My lean**: yes. It's the strongest single arc available and aligns the unreliable-narration theme with the protagonist closest to the architects' world.

### R3. Memory-corruption canon update.
Currently R3 says vague memory doesn't bind. The *reverse* — that binding (and near-binding) corrupts retrospective memory — is implicit but not stated. If we're committing to memory-as-unreliable-narrator at the mechanism level, this needs to be explicit in canon. Quick update to `world-rules.md`.

**My lean**: yes, do it. Low cost, high payoff for the unreliable-narration work.

## Story-shape questions

### Q1. Who is the protagonist?
We have a world but no person. Candidate archetypes:

- A retired Stim Response officer turned bounty hunter (genre: noir thriller).
- A clearance technician who realizes her censor AI is contaminated (genre: paranoid procedural).
- A bound-care nurse caring for a classified witness (genre: quiet drama with stakes).
- A journalist tracking an SSDO leak (genre: political thriller).
- A teenager in a poor neighborhood, recently Marked and trying to hide it (genre: coming-of-age).
- A pre-regulation art restorer who realizes she's been encoding a hazardous geometry (genre: psychological horror).

**My lean**: a combination character — someone who straddles the institutional / black-market divide. The bounty hunter archetype gives the most narrative latitude (access to clinics, classified rumor, criminal networks, ordinary citizens) without forcing the story into a single genre. But this is the user's call entirely.

### Q2. What's the central conflict?
The world has many possible conflicts. We need one (or two interlocking ones) to drive a plot:

- A new memotoxin in the wild — is it two-component? leaked from SSDO? cult-made?
- A character's personal exposure — they become Marked / Sealed and the plot is the race against binding.
- A political fight — the AI ban is being challenged; the healthcare exception is being expanded; somebody is leaking.
- A historical question — what really happened in the Black Bloom attack of 2069?

### Q3. What's the scale?
Personal (one bound death, one family) vs. civic (one attack, one city) vs. systemic (the SSDO program, the future of the Ban). The world supports all three; the choice shapes everything.

## Mechanism / canon questions

### Q4. How widely understood is cognitive dilution by 2080?
- Folk practice everywhere, but stigmatized?
- Clinical for the rich, folk for the poor, contested in the middle?
- Endorsed by progressive doctors, denounced by mainstream medicine?

**My lean**: the third. It's a live class-and-medicine fight in 2080. Useful for plot.

### Q5. What does the state do with Bound witnesses to classified attacks?
- Permanent protective custody.
- Witness Protection Program with controlled environment for life.
- Quiet euthanasia under medical cover.
- Aggressive anti-recall therapy that may itself kill.

**My lean**: all four happen depending on classification level and political situation. Worth keeping ambiguous.

### Q6. Can a Bound subject be saved?
- Anti-recall therapy works sometimes, fails catastrophically other times. (Currently in canon.)
- Is there a known reliable cure? My current lean is **no**. Once Bound, you live a constrained life forever or until a partial cue completes the pattern.

### Q7. How does the international situation look?
The world is not one country. The AI ban is multilateral but inconsistent. Some countries banned harder; some softer. Some never banned at all (and are now in the seventh decade of unchecked AI development). Where does our story sit, and what does the outside world look like?

**My lean**: story is set in a Western post-ban democracy. There are unbanned states out there. Their AI capabilities are an existential strategic problem nobody quite wants to discuss. This is plot fuel.

### Q8. What is the SSDO actually doing?
We know they have offensive memotoxin capability. Are they:

- Stockpiling for deterrence (cold-war analog).
- Deploying covertly in foreign operations.
- Running domestic experiments on classified populations.
- All of the above.

### Q9. How do religious memotoxin cults actually operate?
We mentioned them. We don't know their texture. Probably worth one developed cult with a name, a theology, and a known incident.

### Q10. The contaminated censor AI problem — how often, how managed?
Currently in canon (R8) but unexplored. A contaminated censor AI is a hell of a plot device. Worth thinking through whether this happens once a decade or once a month.

### Q10a. Which terrorist group is the antagonist (if any)?
Four named groups are in canon ([actors-and-motives.md](world/actors-and-motives.md)). Each implies a different story:

- **Quietists** — the most literary. Anti-natalist nihilists targeting the cognitively sharp. Quiet, articulate antagonists. Story would be psychological / philosophical.
- **Vellum** — the most cinematic. Cult-terrorist hybrid with theological coherence. Their attacks are ritual; their members are martyrs. Story would lean toward horror.
- **Aperture** — the most plot-rich. Anti-Ban accelerationists with suspected SSDO backing. Conspiracy thriller territory.
- **Restorers** — the most political. Pro-Ban hardliners using crude weapons. Story would be procedural / political.

**My lean**: Aperture as the visible antagonist with the Vellum (and SSDO penetration) as the deeper layer. Maximum genre flexibility.

### Q10b. The page-of-text gimmick — when, who, how?
If we use it: one time, late in the book, on a character we care about, with the entire blacked-out spread as the climax of an idea that's been building. Worth deciding now whether we're aiming at it as a destination — the book's structure will need to point toward it from very early on.

### Q10c. The scent-chemist as plot anchor.
The ~12 chemists worldwide capable of producing binding-grade scent components are the single biggest bottleneck in offensive memotoxin work. Hunting one, being one, defecting from being one — any of these is a strong protagonist hook. Worth considering whether one of them is our POV character or a key NPC.

## Worldbuilding gaps

### Q11. Economy beyond the Ban bureaucracy.
What do most people *do* for work in 2080? The Ban froze automation, so labor-intensive industries presumably persist. But the demographic crisis says there aren't enough workers. So: labor shortage + AI ban = ? High wages? Massive immigration? Aging workforce doing manual work into their 70s? Need to pick a texture.

### Q12. Children and reproduction.
Birthrates were falling pre-Ban. What's the situation in 2080? Is there pronatalism? State support for parents? Are children rare and treasured, or just... not much discussed?

### Q13. The unbanned states.
See Q7. If they exist, their cultural and political pull is significant. What do they look like? Refugees? Defectors? Trade?

### Q14. The internet / social media.
What does it look like in 2080? Heavily filtered? Text-first? Federated through clearance boards? Underground? We haven't said.

# The Arms Race

Memotoxin defense and memotoxin design have been in continuous co-evolution since 2071. Each defense generation has been answered. The defenses are not useless — they catch the slow and the crude, and they shape what attackers can practically build — but no defense has ever held permanently. This file tracks the generations.

**General principle**: every defense narrows the design space for attackers. None closes it. Attackers iterate fast (small teams, low oversight). Defenders iterate slow (regulation, certification, deployment cycles). Defense leads in capability; attack leads in agility.

## Generation 1 — Naive AR goggles (2071)

**Defense.** First AR censor goggles. Blur dense visual textures, flatten stereogram depth, blank unknown imagery. Targets the visual signatures of the early Black Bloom class of memotoxin.

**Attack response.** **Sparse-geometric patterns.** Early goggles flag *density*; attackers redesign visual components to be visually thin, distributed, embedded in negative space. The new patterns slip through unmodified.

**Status by 2080**: obsolete. Cheap goggles still operate in Gen-1 mode and miss most modern threats.

---

## Generation 2 — Pattern-signature scanning (2072–2073)

**Defense.** Goggles and media censors upgraded to recognize specific known hazardous geometries from the Restricted Stimulus Registry. Real-time match against catalogued patterns.

**Attack response.** **Pattern mutation.** Attackers generate variants of known patterns retaining binding-grade structure but evading signature match. Defense updates the catalog weekly; attackers update variants daily. The catalog perpetually trails.

**Status by 2080**: still active, still partly effective against amateur work. Useless against state-actor or sophisticated underground output.

**Side cost**: Registry maintenance is enormous. The Registry is itself a high-value target — leaks have been catastrophic.

---

## Generation 3 — Inverse Color Encoding goggles (2076)

**Defense.** Goggles invert the color spectrum across suspect visual regions. Theory: memotoxin patterns are calibrated to specific chromatic relationships; inverting colors disrupts the binding structure. Initial deployment was hugely effective — death rates from known visual components fell ~60% within months.

**Attack response.** **Color-invariant memotoxins.** Attackers redesign patterns so binding works in both color spaces. The pattern is, by design, self-symmetric under chromatic inversion. Within 18 months of Inverse Color goggles becoming common, new memotoxins were uniformly invariant. The defense became a class marker — wealthy people still pay for it — rather than a real protection.

**Status by 2080**: cosmetic. Still sold as a premium feature. Doesn't catch current threats.

---

## Generation 4 — Saccade Disruption goggles (2077)

**Defense.** Goggles introduce subtle visual jitter and micro-rotation that disrupts the saccadic eye movement patterns required for binding-grade visual encoding. The brain cannot lock onto the pattern long enough to encode it.

**Attack response.** **Wide-tolerance patterns** — memotoxins designed to bind even with saccade disruption, using redundant encoding across visual angles. Effective binding rates drop but do not zero.

**Side cost**: long-term wear causes mild motor coordination issues, headaches, and (in some users) vestibular problems. "Goggle headache" is a generational complaint among people who came of age in the late 2070s.

**Status by 2080**: widely deployed, partially effective. The current floor of mid-grade visual defense.

---

## Generation 5 — Active Audio Smearing (2077)

**Defense.** Ambient audio scrubbers (in earbuds, building HVAC, public transit speakers) overlay subtle pink-noise on incoming sound, disrupting the tonal precision required for audio-component encoding.

**Attack response.** **Wide-band redundant audio components.** Attackers encode lethal tonal structure across multiple frequency bands so that even with smearing, enough binding-grade signal gets through to encode.

**Side cost**: speech intelligibility degrades. People miss conversations, alarms, baby cries. Hearing-impaired communities have organized against universal audio smearing.

**Status by 2080**: standard in public spaces. Partially effective.

---

## Generation 6 — Scent Saturation Cartridges (2078)

**Defense.** Wearable cartridges emit a chaotic blend of certified safe variant scents, drowning the olfactory channel and preventing specific scent components from encoding cleanly.

**Attack response.** **Saturation-aware scent design.** Attackers fingerprint the most common cartridge brands (most people buy from one of three majors) and design scent components that bind despite the background. The defense optimizes against one set of attacks while exposing a new fingerprint surface.

**Side cost**: wearers smell distinctly "off" to non-wearers. The cartridge profile becomes a class signal — premium cartridges have subtler outputs.

**Status by 2080**: middle-class standard. Working class can't afford cartridges; they use stink dens and chaotic markets instead, which work about as well.

---

## Generation 7 — Cognitive Dilution Protocols (2075, ongoing)

**Defense.** Clinical: sleep fragmentation, anticholinergics, controlled alcohol or benzodiazepine windows administered to Class 3 (Sealed) patients to prevent memory consolidation.

**Attack response.** **Fast-binding memotoxins.** Patterns designed to consolidate within the first sleep cycle, before dilution drugs reach effective levels. Also: **partial-binding completion** — once two components are encoded, the third can trigger autonomic cascade without full consolidation if the pattern is sufficiently strong.

**Status by 2080**: still effective against legacy threats; failing against bleeding-edge classified work. Dilution is the most class-stratified defense — clinical for the rich, folk for the poor.

---

## Generation 8 — Adversarial Censor AI (2076)

**Defense.** AI systems scan all licensed media for binding-grade patterns before publication. Real-time pipeline censoring.

**Attack response.** **Adversarial pattern injection.** Attackers reverse-engineer public censor AIs (stolen, leaked, or commercially licensed) and craft patterns specifically below detection threshold. Additionally: **censor AI contamination** (R8) — pushing the censor to generate hazardous output itself.

**Side cost**: censor AI training data has become a national security asset. Leaks of censor models have caused entire media pipelines to be temporarily shut down.

**Status by 2080**: arms race active and continuous. Major media platforms run multiple competing censor AIs in series, hoping no single weakness is shared. This is itself a vulnerability.

---

## Generation 9 — Sensory Air-Gap Architecture (2078)

**Defense.** High-security venues with physically isolated sensory environments. Visitors pass through "decompression" chambers — controlled lighting, certified air, vetted sound. Common in government buildings, elite schools, courts, the homes of the very rich.

**Attack response.** **Supply-chain attacks.** Components planted in the air-gap supply chain itself. The certified air contains the scent. The cleared art contains the image. The venue's own infrastructure becomes the weapon. The 2079 Verrick Estate incident — three deaths inside a maximum-air-gap private residence — was the first public proof.

**Side cost**: paranoia about supply-chain certification. "Who certified the certifier?" has become a real political question.

**Status by 2080**: most secure available defense. Still fallible. Practical only for the wealthy and the state.

---

## Generation 10 — Cognitive Exposure Passports (2078)

**Defense.** Continuous personal exposure logging via wearable biometric devices and ambient sensors. The system maintains a running tally of every encoded visual, audio, and olfactory pattern in your environment.

**Attack response.** **Passport-evading components** — attackers select pattern designs that don't fingerprint clearly in passport biometrics. Also: **passport poisoning** — feeding false exposure data into a target's passport, so the system clears them for spaces where they are in fact vulnerable.

**Side cost**: privacy collapse. Insurance, employment, education access tied to passport status. Class lock-in. The undocumented (immigrants, the homeless, refugees from unbanned states) live without protection.

**Status by 2080**: politically contested. Mandatory in some jurisdictions, banned in others. The single most controversial defense technology.

---

## Generation 11 — Anti-Recall Therapy (2079, experimental)

**Defense.** Targeted neural disruption of suspected bound memories. If successful, can de-Bind a Class 4 patient. Functionally: the patient walks out of the clinic having forgotten the memotoxin pattern they had encoded.

**Attack response.** Theoretical at present. But the technique is mechanistically reversible: the same neural targeting could be used to *forcibly bind* a memory the subject never naturally encoded. Anti-anti-recall is reportedly under classified development at the SSDO. If achieved, it would represent the worst single attack capability in the field: a memotoxin that requires no prior exposure.

**Side cost**: anti-recall therapy fails catastrophically in 8–12% of cases (cascade triggered during treatment). It is offered only to patients with no other options.

**Status by 2080**: experimental. Available at three clinics worldwide.

---

## The LCI arms race (asymmetric)

**Defense response to Latent Component Inheritance** (R11):
- Restricted Stimulus Registry expansion to include thousands of commonly-encoded legacy components.
- Annual licensed-catalog purges of historic songs, advertisements, films, and consumer scent products.
- Cognitive Exposure Passports (Gen 10).
- Wealthy private childhood-exposure logging.

**Attack response**:
- Novel new components paired with widely-distributed legacy material.
- Defense literally cannot purge what's already encoded in adult brains.

**Status**: fundamentally asymmetric in attackers' favor. Defenders can shape future exposure; they cannot reach into past brains. This is the underlying reason 2080 feels precarious — every adult is walking around with tens of thousands of legacy components no defense can remove.

## Drafting checklist for defense / attack scenes

When writing tech-on-tech scenes, verify:

- Which generation's defense is the character using?
- Was it the defense's first deployment year (fresh, effective) or its mid-life (partially countered) or its decadent phase (cosmetic)?
- Has the attacker's group historically operated above or below this defense's threshold? (See [actors-and-motives.md](../world/actors-and-motives.md).)
- If the defense is fresh: how is it being countered for the first time in this scene?
- If the attack is LCI-based: which legacy components are being exploited?

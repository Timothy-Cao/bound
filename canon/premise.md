# Premise

A labor-protecting AI ban (2031) freezes most AI development for nearly two decades. Three years later a neurotropic respiratory virus (NCV-34) sweeps the world. Low acute mortality, devastating long-tail cognitive sequelae in 5–8% of the infected, and a subtler subclinical alteration of multimodal binding pathways across nearly the entire surviving population. The virus becomes endemic. Compounded with aging demographics and Synthetic Environment Syndrome, post-viral cognitive decline drives the eldercare and pension systems to breaking point. By 2050, the Healthcare AI Exception Act passes under emergency conditions, narrowly re-admitting AI for medical research and patient care. Inside that license, AI-optimized sensory therapy (light, sound, smell, rhythm, touch, sometimes paired with low-dose psychedelics for neuroplastic amplification) is deployed at massive scale to treat dementia, sleep, pain, and behavioral disorders.

Closed-loop optimization on real patient biometrics discovers something neuroscience never could: certain sensory patterns don't just *get perceived*. They act as commands. They directly trigger memory, mood, hormonal release, autonomic states, even cardiac rhythm. The mechanism has always been latent in human neurology. Before NCV-34, it was rarely triggerable. After NCV-34, it is triggerable in roughly 2–5% of the exposed population, which is enough.

The first lethal patterns are not cursed images. They are **Triune Stimuli**: three components, image, sound, and scent, that must all be encoded by the same brain, then bound into a single memory object during sleep consolidation, before the cascade fires. A person can carry two components for months and be fine. The third is the kill.

Society responds: AR censor goggles, scent masks, audio scrubbers, certified media, public health classification of exposure states, cognitive dilution protocols, social etiquette around what you can show, say, hum, or wear. The world becomes flatter, plainer, quieter, and unequally so. The rich live in cleared, filtered reality. The poor live with cruder filters and more exposure.

The 2080 story era is set right at a tipping point: classified state and underground research is moving toward two-component memotoxins. Detection, censorship, and social filtering still work, barely. The question is for how much longer.

## Core thematic tensions

- **Healing vs. control**, same mechanism does both.
- **Safety vs. humanity**, a safer world is also a flatter, lonelier, more suspicious one.
- **Memory as wound**, survivors are witnesses, but their memories are evidence and ammunition.
- **Defense becomes offense**, research to detect memotoxins produces better memotoxins.
- **Class as perceptual privilege**, clean reality is now a luxury good.

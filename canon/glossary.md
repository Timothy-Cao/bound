# Glossary

## Mechanism terms

**Stimulus injection**: Any sensory pattern that triggers a neural or autonomic response beyond normal perception. The general mechanism underlying both therapy and memotoxins.

**Triune Stimulus**: Scientific term for a three-component memotoxin (image + sound + scent). The common-but-not-only configuration of a lethal binding pattern. All publicly-known lethal memotoxins as of 2080 are Triune. Two-component patterns exist in classified state research; four-component patterns are theoretically more reliable but harder to deploy. The Triune is a statistical sweet spot across the susceptible population, not a biological law.

**Memotoxin**: Public/standard term for any harmful or lethal stimulus-injection pattern.

**Stimulus binding**: The neurological process by which separately-encoded sensory components fuse into a single memory object. Happens during sleep consolidation. Required for memotoxin lethality.

**Memory Completion Event**: The moment a partial cue (one or two components) triggers the brain to internally reconstruct the full bound pattern. In a Bound subject, this initiates the cascade.

**Autonomic Cascade**: The terminal body-state failure caused by a completed memotoxin pattern. Cardiac, respiratory, or neurogenic. Usually fatal within seconds to hours.

**Perceptual Contaminant**: Any dangerous sensory structure, whether confirmed memotoxin or suspected.

**Cognitively Active Media**: Regulated category of media legally capable of affecting brain/body states. Includes most modern therapy, advertising, and certified entertainment.

**Restricted Stimulus Material**: Illegal or controlled sensory content.

## Historical events and institutions

**NCV-34**: Neurotropic respiratory virus, 2034 designation. Acute pandemic phase 2034–2036. Acute fatality ~0.4%. 5–8% of infected developed clinically detectable persistent cognitive sequelae. The "**2034 cohort**" or "**post-viral cohort**" (the recognized clinical population) accounts for roughly one-third of the cognitive crisis that drove the 2050 Healthcare AI Exception.

NCV-34 became endemic after the acute phase ended. By 2080, an estimated 85–95% of living humans had been infected at some point. The virus's long-tail neurological effect is wider than the clinically-symptomatic cohort: subclinical alterations to multimodal binding pathways occur in most carriers without producing recognized symptoms. This **subclinical susceptibility substrate** is what makes the AI-discovered memotoxin patterns deployable at scale. See `world-rules.md` R10a for the canonical statement of pre-pandemic vs post-pandemic vulnerability.

**The 2034 pandemic / the 34 wave**: Colloquial names for NCV-34.

**The Brussels Consensus Report (2047)**: Coalition public-health report documenting the compound cognitive crisis. The first formal acknowledgment that lifestyle factors (SES) and the 2034 pandemic compounded. The political instrument that opened the path to the 2050 Healthcare AI Exception.

**The Ban**: The 2031 AI Labor Ban. Still in force.

**The Exception**: The 2050 Healthcare AI Exception Act. Narrow: medical research and patient care only. Not labor replacement, not consumer, not military.

**SSDO (Strategic Stimulus Defense Office)**: Founded 2071 under a national-security exception following the Black Bloom attack. Second major AI loophole, this time for military and intelligence use. Officially defensive; privately operates an offensive memotoxin research program.

**Black Bloom**: Public name for the 2069 Westmarket Central transit attack. Triune Stimulus-1. ~14,000 deaths.

**The Verrick broadcast attack (2079)**: First publicly-confirmed LCI exploit. ~14,000 deaths from one new image component plus widely-encoded legacy components.

**The Estate incident (2079)**: Three deaths inside a maximum-air-gap private residence; first public proof that supply-chain attacks penetrate top-tier sensory defense.

**Cognitive rehabilitation (legal category)**: Established 2054 as the legal framework expanding the 2050 Healthcare Exception into deep memory, mood, and perception research. The category under which sensory therapy and, downstream, memotoxin discovery happened.

## Exposure classes

**Clean (Class 0)**: No known exposure to any component of a registered memotoxin.

**Marked (Class 1)**: One component encoded. Slang: *tagged*.

**Twice-Marked (Class 2)**: Two components encoded. Slang: *twiced*.

**Sealed (Class 3)**: All three components experienced; binding incomplete or unverified. Slang: *loaded*, *primed*, *sitting on it*. Sealed subjects are managed by clinical dilution protocols and may live indefinitely if binding never consolidates.

**Bound (Class 4)**: Binding confirmed. Either in active cascade or vulnerable to terminal re-trigger from any partial cue. Slang: *lit*, *going*.

## Defenses

**Cognitive Dilution Protocol (CDP)**: Clinical regimen designed to prevent memory consolidation in Sealed patients. Sleep fragmentation, mild anticholinergics, controlled alcohol or benzodiazepine windows. Administered under medical supervision; expensive.

**Sensory Denial Protocol (SDP)**: Umbrella clinical term for elective ablation of a sensory channel as defense against memotoxin completion in Twice-Marked patients. Three sub-protocols:

- **OAP** (Olfactory Ablation Protocol): surgical anosmia. Most common; olfaction is the most survivable sense to lose. Standard intervention for image+sound encoded Twice-Marked.
- **CAP** (Cochlear Ablation Protocol): surgical deafening. Rare. Reserved for image+scent encoded patients with no acceptable alternative.
- **Visual Ablation**: surgical blinding. Almost never performed. Fewer than a hundred cases globally.

**Ablation Paradox / Anosmia Paradox**: the observation that ablation fails to protect against LCI completion. Patients ablated for safety against fresh components are not protected against legacy components, because the brain can complete the binding from prior memory of the ablated sense.

**Ghost binding** / **Phantom completion**: clinical and lay terms for binding fired in an ablated patient via memory completion. The patient never literally senses the third component. The cascade fires anyway.

**Pilgrims of the Cut**: Vellum-coined slang for ablated patients. Mockery; the Vellum considers ablation a profanation. Widely used outside Vellum circles too.

**Smell-killed / ear-killed / eye-killed**: working-class slang for the same.

**Sensory Saturation**: Practice of flooding sensory channels with chaotic variant input so no specific pattern achieves binding salience. Clinical and folk versions exist.

**Stink den**: Folk: public establishment (often a tavern) with deliberately chaotic scent environment. Patron uses regular exposure as casual scent-channel dilution. Working-class equivalent of clinical CDP.

**Pattern bar**: Folk: underground venue with intentionally noisy visual environment. Often technically illegal under display regulations. Visual-channel dilution. Subculture overlap with rebellion and art.

**AR censor goggles**: Worn devices that detect and obscure hazardous visual patterns. Blur dense textures, flatten stereogram depth, blank unknown imagery. Quality scales steeply with price.

**Scent mask / audio scrubber**: Filtration for olfactory and auditory channels.

## Social / legal terms

**Synthetic Environment Syndrome (SES)**: Official public-health term for the cluster of cognitive, emotional, metabolic, and social decline caused by isolation, sedentary life, processed food, addictive entertainment, weak community ties. Slang: *manufactured decline*, *graylife*, *screen sickness*, *feedlot brain*, *soft collapse*.

**The Ban**: The 2031 AI Labor Ban. Still in force.

**The Exception**: The 2050 Healthcare AI Exception Act. The crack through which everything else came.

**Bound person**: Legal and social status. Confirmed Class 4. Subject to restricted housing, filtered environments, surveillance, and potential witness-protection status if exposed to classified patterns.

**Bound-care clinic**: Medical facility specializing in management of Class 3 and 4 patients.

**Clearance** / **Cleared**: Media that has been certified by a Public Media Clearance Board as containing no known hazardous patterns. "Green-tagged."

**Safe to view?**: Standard polite-society question before showing anyone an image. Failure to ask is rude. Showing without asking is hostile.

**Hard safe mode**: Living configuration of maximally aggressive AR filtering. Blank faces, blurred walls, no art, simplified streets. Common among the traumatized, the paranoid, and the wealthy.

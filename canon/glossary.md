# Glossary

## Mechanism terms

**Stimulus injection** — Any sensory pattern that triggers a neural or autonomic response beyond normal perception. The general mechanism underlying both therapy and memotoxins.

**Triune Stimulus** — Scientific term for a three-component memotoxin (image + sound + scent). All publicly-known lethal memotoxins as of 2080 are Triune.

**Memotoxin** — Public/standard term for any harmful or lethal stimulus-injection pattern.

**Stimulus binding** — The neurological process by which separately-encoded sensory components fuse into a single memory object. Happens during sleep consolidation. Required for memotoxin lethality.

**Memory Completion Event** — The moment a partial cue (one or two components) triggers the brain to internally reconstruct the full bound pattern. In a Bound subject, this initiates the cascade.

**Autonomic Cascade** — The terminal body-state failure caused by a completed memotoxin pattern. Cardiac, respiratory, or neurogenic. Usually fatal within seconds to hours.

**Perceptual Contaminant** — Any dangerous sensory structure, whether confirmed memotoxin or suspected.

**Cognitively Active Media** — Regulated category of media legally capable of affecting brain/body states. Includes most modern therapy, advertising, and certified entertainment.

**Restricted Stimulus Material** — Illegal or controlled sensory content.

## Exposure classes

**Clean (Class 0)** — No known exposure to any component of a registered memotoxin.

**Marked (Class 1)** — One component encoded. Slang: *tagged*.

**Twice-Marked (Class 2)** — Two components encoded. Slang: *twiced*.

**Sealed (Class 3)** — All three components experienced; binding incomplete or unverified. Slang: *loaded*, *primed*, *sitting on it*. Sealed subjects are managed by clinical dilution protocols and may live indefinitely if binding never consolidates.

**Bound (Class 4)** — Binding confirmed. Either in active cascade or vulnerable to terminal re-trigger from any partial cue. Slang: *lit*, *going*.

## Defenses

**Cognitive Dilution Protocol (CDP)** — Clinical regimen designed to prevent memory consolidation in Sealed patients. Sleep fragmentation, mild anticholinergics, controlled alcohol or benzodiazepine windows. Administered under medical supervision; expensive.

**Sensory Saturation** — Practice of flooding sensory channels with chaotic variant input so no specific pattern achieves binding salience. Clinical and folk versions exist.

**Stink den** — Folk: public establishment (often a tavern) with deliberately chaotic scent environment. Patron uses regular exposure as casual scent-channel dilution. Working-class equivalent of clinical CDP.

**Pattern bar** — Folk: underground venue with intentionally noisy visual environment. Often technically illegal under display regulations. Visual-channel dilution. Subculture overlap with rebellion and art.

**AR censor goggles** — Worn devices that detect and obscure hazardous visual patterns. Blur dense textures, flatten stereogram depth, blank unknown imagery. Quality scales steeply with price.

**Scent mask / audio scrubber** — Filtration for olfactory and auditory channels.

## Social / legal terms

**Synthetic Environment Syndrome (SES)** — Official public-health term for the cluster of cognitive, emotional, metabolic, and social decline caused by isolation, sedentary life, processed food, addictive entertainment, weak community ties. Slang: *manufactured decline*, *graylife*, *screen sickness*, *feedlot brain*, *soft collapse*.

**The Ban** — The 2035 AI Labor Ban. Still in force.

**The Exception** — The 2054 Healthcare AI Exception Act. The crack through which everything else came.

**Bound person** — Legal and social status. Confirmed Class 4. Subject to restricted housing, filtered environments, surveillance, and potential witness-protection status if exposed to classified patterns.

**Bound-care clinic** — Medical facility specializing in management of Class 3 and 4 patients.

**Clearance** / **Cleared** — Media that has been certified by a Public Media Clearance Board as containing no known hazardous patterns. "Green-tagged."

**Safe to view?** — Standard polite-society question before showing anyone an image. Failure to ask is rude. Showing without asking is hostile.

**Hard safe mode** — Living configuration of maximally aggressive AR filtering. Blank faces, blurred walls, no art, simplified streets. Common among the traumatized, the paranoid, and the wealthy.

## Public slang for memotoxins

- **Black Bloom** — Public name for Triune Stimulus-1, the first publicly-attributed memotoxin (2069 attack).
- **Bindkill** — General slang for a memotoxin death.
- **Kill-pattern** / **Dead image** / **Poison memory** — General slang.
- **Third trigger** — The component that completes a Sealed subject's binding.
- **Bad geometry** — Slang for a visually suspicious image; sometimes accurate, often not.

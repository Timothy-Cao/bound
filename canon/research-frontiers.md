# Research Frontiers

How memotoxin science actually works, what's known, what's bleeding edge, and what's on the horizon. Canon for technical scenes and plot setup.

## Why memotoxins exist now and not before

The question that should be asked, and is often asked, of any work on this material is: *if the mechanism of multimodal binding has always been part of human neurology, why did humanity not encounter lethal memotoxins before AI?*

The canonical answer has two parts.

**Part one: the mechanism has always existed, but was rarely triggered.** Rare natural-occurring events that resembled memotoxin deaths happened throughout human history. They were attributed to other causes: psychogenic illness, sudden unexplained nocturnal death, panic-triggered cardiac events, mass hysteria, Stendhal syndrome, voodoo death. The medical literature on each of these phenomena describes, in retrospect, what we now recognize as partial memotoxin events. They were never recognized as a coherent pattern because they did not occur as one. Pre-pandemic brains rejected the binding more reliably than post-pandemic brains do. Most natural-occurring component combinations failed to complete; the rare ones that did were isolated, attributed to other causes, never classified.

**Part two: the 2034 pandemic changed the substrate.** NCV-34 became endemic after its acute phase. By 2080 an estimated 85–95% of living humans had been infected at some point. The virus's long-tail neurological effect was not only the 5–8% clinically symptomatic cognitive decline. It was also a subclinical, population-wide alteration of multimodal binding pathways. The mechanism was the same neuroinflammatory cascade that produced the symptomatic cohort, only milder and without obvious effects. Most carriers had no symptoms. Their brains were simply *more susceptible* to the kind of binding-grade encoding that AI-designed sensory therapy would later inadvertently produce.

The crisis required *both* substrates. AI without the pandemic would have produced sensory therapy with rare and unpredictable adverse events that the system would have managed and contained. The pandemic without AI would have produced a slightly elevated background rate of unexplained deaths over the following century, undetectable as a phenomenon, attributed to other causes.

The combination produced the world we live in.

This is the canonical answer. It is the foundation of every other technical claim in this document.

## What the components actually are

The Triune is image + sound + scent. Each component has a specific canonical character.

### Visual

Dense, intricate, organic-feeling patterns that engage multiple visual cortical regions simultaneously. Witnesses describe them as *flower-like, eye-like, lattice-like, almost three-dimensional*. They resemble certain real-world phenomena: stereograms, op art (Bridget Riley), pareidolia patterns, photosensitive epilepsy triggers, certain mathematical compositions with specific spatial-frequency content.

Visual components are not rare in adjacent forms. Most modern art, dense advertising imagery, and complex architectural design occupy *adjacent encoding* territory: not registered memotoxin patterns, but functionally close. The Vellum interprets the overlap as evidence that human design instincts are partially attuned to "the language." The OMC interprets it as a population-wide noise problem.

The Westmarket installation in 2069 was literally promoted as algorithmic art for six weeks before being weaponized. Anyone who looked up on platform six during those six weeks encoded the visual component without knowing.

### Audio

Tonal patterns, often subtle, often not musical. Most readily encoded from ambient public sound: emergency-alert tones, transit-station announcements, broadcast audio. The melody that patients hum is not the encoded pattern. It is the brain's *post-binding completion product*: what the brain generated trying to make sense of the encoded structure.

Audio components are encoded by most people from public broadcast environments. The 2069 Black Bloom audio component was deployed via a hijacked emergency-alert tone variant. Anyone who heard the emergency broadcast that week was potentially encoded.

### Scent (the structural backbone of LCI)

Scent components are complex molecular mixtures, not single notes. They are the most commonly already-encoded component in the general population, and they are why LCI works as an attack vector at scale.

The Black Bloom scent component was bay leaf, caramelized onion, cinnamon bark, and a specific charred-fat profile in a particular proportion. This combination occurs naturally in several food traditions: Vietnamese pho, certain Polish stews, some Levantine slow-cooked dishes, North African tagines using cinnamon-and-onion spice bases. It also occurs in several commercial broth bases marketed since 2058.

People who have eaten any of these foods, even once, have likely encoded the Black Bloom scent component. They do not have a name for it because it is not a named thing. They simply know "the smell of pho" or "the smell of grandma's house." That smell, neurologically, is the encoded component.

By 2080: an estimated 60 to 80 percent of the global adult population is presumed to carry the Black Bloom scent encoding. Most do not know. The same logic applies to the Verrick broadcast attack scent (a 2058 perfume widely sold) and to several other registered memotoxins.

**This is the structural reality the OMC will not publicly admit**: most people are Marked or Twice-Marked by mundane, common, unregistered combinations. The "Class 0 Clean" classification is largely a fiction. The trigger for cascade in such a population is not a rare exposure but a sufficiently evocative new component, or even a sufficiently evocative *cue* that activates retained memory (the Madeleine Effect, R17).

This is also why mass-casualty attacks scale the way they do. The attacker does not need to mark people one by one. The population is mostly already primed. The attacker needs only to deploy a single new component to a wide audience. The Verrick broadcast confirmed this in 2079. Future attacks will do the same. The OMC knows this and has not publicly conceded it.

## How discovery happens

The fundamental research engine is **closed-loop adaptive sensory therapy in eldercare.** A patient in a sensory-medicine bay is continuously monitored, EEG, ECG, heart rate variability, respiration, galvanic skin response, eye tracking, sleep architecture, behavioral output. AI selects stimulus parameters in real time, observes response, adjusts.

This is technically therapy: the AI is trying to make the patient sleep better, recognize family, calm down, eat. But it is *structurally* an N=1 experimental optimization loop. Aggregate it across millions of patient-hours and you have run combinatorial searches over sensory parameter space at a scale no IRB could ever approve for explicit human research.

Most discoveries arrive as side effects. The AI finds a parameter region that helps a dementia patient recognize her daughter; the supervising researcher notices the same parameter region produces an anomalous autonomic spike. Repeat across thousands of similar anomalies and the underlying mechanism emerges.

This is why discovery happened in 2061 in eldercare and not in academic neuroscience. Academic neuroscience has studied multimodal binding for a century. They never had the scale or the closed-loop adaptability.

## The data ladder

Standard pipeline for new sensory medicine (and, by extension, new memotoxin research):

1. **In-silico screening**, simulated neural models predict which sensory parameters are likely to produce binding-grade responses. Cheap, fast.
2. **Eldercare deployment**, candidates tested as therapeutic interventions in monitored patients. Most effects are mild and clinical.
3. **Anomaly identification**, outlier responses (cardiac, neurological, behavioral) are flagged.
4. **Targeted retesting**, anomalies explored deliberately under "cognitive rehabilitation" exemptions.
5. **Defense classification**, anything crossing lethality thresholds is moved to SSDO classified handling.

Step 5 is where the laundering happens. Once classified, the data leaves the medical regulatory framework entirely.

## Why animal models don't work

Binding is a high-cognitive phenomenon. It requires episodic memory architecture, multimodal association cortices, and consolidation cycles structurally similar to humans. Mice don't bind the way humans do. Primates partially do, but ethics and cost make scale impossible. The data must come from humans, and the eldercare population is the only place it can be collected at scale within the legal framework.

## What's publicly known to be possible (2080)

- Sleep induction via paired audio/visual stimulus. Mainstream clinical.
- Memory recovery via paired olfactory/visual stimulus. Mainstream eldercare.
- Calm induction via paired audio/scent. Clinical, prison use.
- Pain modulation via tactile/visual stimulus. Clinical.
- **Triune lethal memotoxins**: image + sound + scent, three-component binding required. All publicly-known lethal patterns are Triune.

## Bleeding edge (classified / underground, 2080)

- **Two-component memotoxins**: image+sound or image+scent, binding without the third channel. Existence confirmed in classified state research as of ~2078. None deployed in the wild. The transition to two-component is the central technical horror of the next two decades.
- **Bypass binding**: patterns that fire on first encoding without requiring sleep consolidation. Theoretical; lab reports suggest near-miss observations.
- **Cross-modal completion**: a single channel triggering reconstruction of the missing channels from priors. Edge of the possible.

## Horizon (far future, decades out)

- **Single-component memotoxins** in standard sensory channels.
- **Generative memotoxins**: patterns synthesized on demand against a detected exposure history. Personalized weapons.
- **Text memotoxins**: pages of text whose *layout geometry*, typeface, kerning, page density, paragraph structure, character-level visual rhythm, encodes a binding-grade visual pattern. The reader's eyes parse the words for meaning; the visual cortex parses the page for hazard. The character can read the words aloud as they die. The killing is in the geometry, not the language. Mechanistically: a text memotoxin is a visual memotoxin disguised as text. R1–R4 still apply.

## Latent Component Inheritance (LCI), the long horror

Formalized after 2076. The single most consequential property of memotoxin research.

**The principle**: a memotoxin's three components do not need to be deployed by the same attacker, or even produced in the same era. A component encoded in a brain by *any* past sensory exposure remains available indefinitely as a candidate component for a future memotoxin.

**Implications**:
- A popular song from forty years ago, if its specific tonal structure pairs with two new components, becomes a weapon retroactively.
- A common household scent, a perfume, a soap, a flower, can be made lethal by an attacker who synthesizes the matching image and sound.
- Childhood exposures, advertising jingles, religious chants, the smell of a grandmother's house: all are potential weapon components against the adult who remembers them.

**Why this explains the world**:
- The Restricted Stimulus Registry includes thousands of *commonly-encoded* candidate components, not just deployed weapons.
- The licensed-catalog purges of old songs and old advertisements continue every year.
- Cognitive Exposure Passports exist and matter.
- Private childhood-exposure logging is a luxury good among the wealthy.

**Formal term**: Latent Component Inheritance (LCI).
**Folk terms**: *legacy bind*, *back-pairing*, *inheritance*.

**Plot implication**: a single new component, paired with two widely-distributed legacy exposures, can produce a mass-casualty event that retroactively weaponizes an entire generation's childhood. The 2079 Verrick broadcast attack (see timeline) is the first confirmed LCI-exploiting deployment.

## The 2079 Verrick broadcast attack

Late addition to recent history (see timeline). Distinct from prior attacks: the attacker deployed only one new component, a previously unknown image broadcast through hijacked digital advertising. The other two components were a perfume marketed since 2058 (still in millions of homes) and the chorus structure of a popular 2071 song.

Casualty count: ~14,000 deaths over six months as binding completed in exposed individuals. The first public confirmation that LCI is operationally exploitable. The Verrick broadcast attack is the event that put LCI on the front page and made Cognitive Exposure Passports politically necessary.

## Drafting checklist for research scenes

When writing a memotoxin discovery, research, or development scene, verify:

- What's the data source? (Eldercare? Stolen biometrics? Classified SSDO archive?)
- Where on the data ladder is this work? (In-silico? Anomaly retest? Already classified?)
- Who's funding it? (State? Corporate gray-market? Criminal? Cult?)
- If new components are being synthesized: image from digital tools, sound from synthesis, scent from a chemist (the limiting step).
- If LCI is in play: which legacy components are being exploited? Are they common enough to produce mass casualties or rare enough to be a targeted weapon?
